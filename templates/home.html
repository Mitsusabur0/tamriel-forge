Hello, traveler.

The roads have led you to a curious quarter of the realm, where heroes are introduced by firelight, their deeds copied into vellum, their likeness painted in rich oils, and their voices carried into the rafters by patient bards.

Tavern of Heroes

Begin where all good legends begin: in a loud hall with a warm hearth.

Come, take a seat among mercenaries, pilgrims, and mystery-bound wanderers. The keepers of this tavern will ask after your hero's homeland, scars, loyalties, and secret burdens until a worthy soul stands before us.

Speak plainly, traveler. The fire listens well, and first impressions in this house are known to last for ages.

Monks' Scriptorium

Seek the scribes, for they keep the latest manuscript of every soul worth remembering.

Beyond these doors, ink-stained monks bend over long tables and turn rumor into record. Bring them the bones of a life, and they will return a chronicle fit for a court archive or a campfire confession.

If the tale rings false, ask for a fresh draft. The brothers are patient, though they do sigh when made to scrape clean good parchment.

Painters' Atelier

When the face must be remembered, the painters will see to it.

Here work the portraitists of princes, thieves, and saints alike. They know the language of scars, armor, posture, velvet, ash, and moonlight, and they will set your hero upon the canvas in whatever mood the tale demands.

Stand still if you can. Artists are temperamental folk, and some charge extra for heroes who insist on smoldering dramatically.

Bards' Hall

Come to the hall where old voices give breath to rising legends.

The bards gather here beneath carved beams to rehearse victories, vows, betrayals, and last stands. Place your story in their hands, and they will send it back to you wrapped in cadence, echo, and just enough thunder.

Do not forget to tip them. A bard who feels appreciated can make even a brooding exile sound ten feet tall.

Atlas of Realms

And when memory falters, the archivists will show you the shape of the world.

Shelves groan beneath maps, family lines, faction rolls, and brittle accounts of ancient troubles. Wander here whenever your hero needs firmer roots, truer borders, or the name of an old oath no one dares break lightly.

Mind the dust. The archivists insist it is historically significant.
